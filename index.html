An Introduction to Magic the Gathering


Magic the Gathering is a trading cardgame released in 1993 in the United States by Richard Garfield. The game has since become a huge cultural phenomenon all across the world. In this short entry, I will explain some of the core mechanics of the game and help you understand what its all about.
See the Inspiring Overseer as an example. I will be going over its attributes to give you an idea of how to read a Magic card.

First and foremost, in order to cast spells, you need to pay their mana costs. The symbols on the top right corner indicate what that cost is. On the Inspiring Overseer, you can see 2 symbols. One with a two inside of it and one with a sun-like symbol. The latter must be paid using specifically white mana, while the former can be paid with 2 mana of any color combination.
On the left side, the two lower arrows indicate the types of the cards and its effects within the game. A card with the creature typing also has attack and health which allows it to engage in combat.


To start collecting Magic the Gathering, you may want to either buy physical cards at your local game store, or alternatively start playing on a digital client such as Magic Arena.
