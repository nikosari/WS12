Meet the Colors


Magic has a deeply structured world and one of the cornerstones of this world are its colors of Mana. The five different colors are white, blue, black, red and green. Each of these has its own unique characteristics and principles, and the creatures and people in the world of Magic are aligned accordingly.
In terms of the game, colors are the most important mechanic to learn and understand, as the cards have whats called a color identity, a design principle that determines the type of effects a card of any colors may have. Each color has its weaknesses and strengths.

Click the example cards below to see them larger.

Symbol Name Values Strengths Example
White Structure, harmony, peace and equality Auras, strong removal, board wipes, healing
Blue Knowledge, science, exceptionality and technology Card draw, counterspells, deck manipulation, flying creatures
Black Ambition, death, sacrifice and self-interest Removal, reanimation effects, hand disruption and using life as a resource
Red Impulsiveness, fire, action and freedom Non-combat damage, haste creatures and conditional card draw
Green Nature, spirituality and survival of the fittest Mana ramp and searching for lands, huge creatures, making creatures bigger and enchantments

As you may see, each color is truly unique. However its important to remember you can also make color combinations with any amount of colors, even using all five if you so wish. This allows for a lot of creativity in deck building and card design, as many cards also have multiple colors in their mana costs.
